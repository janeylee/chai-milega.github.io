As a fellow with the University of Chicago's International Innovation Corps , one of our team's project goals was to understand mindsets and behaviors pertaining to household waste management. We soon learned that getting people to manage their waste more sustainably was, at its core, a behavior change issue, and started brainstorming ways to ensure its implementation at the apartment level.

Inspired by the 2015 World Bank Development Report, which focused on behavior change in the development context, our team conducted a month-long pilot testing several hypotheses about behaviors around waste management.

As a side note, I happen to believe strongly that sometimes work and play are the same thing, and this case study is the perfect example of that philosophy in action.
